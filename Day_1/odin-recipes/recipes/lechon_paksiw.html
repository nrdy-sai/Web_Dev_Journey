Lechon Paksiw Recipe

Lechon Paksiw is a Filipino dish made from leftover lechon (roast pig) that is simmered in a flavorful sauce made with vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves. It is a delicious way to use up any leftover lechon and is often served with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover lechon, chopped
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the chopped lechon, vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, black peppercorns, and water.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30 minutes to 1 hour, or until the flavors have melded together and the sauce has thickened slightly.
  3. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking. If the sauce reduces too much, you can add a little more water as needed.
  4. Once the flavors have melded together and the sauce has thickened to your liking, remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving.
  5. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
