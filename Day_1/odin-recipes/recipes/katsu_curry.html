Katsu Curry Recipe

Katsu Curry is a Japanese dish that consists of breaded and fried pork cutlet (tonkatsu) served with a flavorful curry sauce. The curry sauce is typically made with a combination of onions, carrots, potatoes, and a blend of spices. It is a comforting and delicious meal that is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless pork chops
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 potatoes, diced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup curry powder
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Season the pork chops with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Dredge each pork chop in the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat it in the panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, fry the breaded pork chops for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and drain on paper towels.
  5. In a separate pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and potatoes, and sauté until they start to soften, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add the chicken broth, curry powder, soy sauce, and honey to the pot. Stir well to combine and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the sauce has thickened.
  7. Serve the fried pork cutlets over steamed rice and ladle the curry sauce on top. Enjoy!
